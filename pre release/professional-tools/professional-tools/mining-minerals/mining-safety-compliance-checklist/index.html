FAQ

Who regulates mine safety in India?

Directorate General of Mines Safety (DGMS), under Ministry of Labour & Employment, is the statutory authority. DGMS inspectors conduct periodic inspections and can issue closure orders for unsafe mines.

What is the penalty for violating Mines Act provisions?

Violations attract fines up to ₹1 lakh and/or imprisonment up to 6 months for first offence; higher for repeat violations. Fatal accidents require mandatory inquiry by DGMS.

What is Section 23 of the Mines Act?

Section 23 empowers the Inspector of Mines to order stoppage of operations or withdrawal of workers in any part of a mine if he believes a danger to life or safety exists. Compliance is immediate and mandatory.
