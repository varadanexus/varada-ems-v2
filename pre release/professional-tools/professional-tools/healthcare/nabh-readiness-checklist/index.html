FAQ

What is NABH accreditation?

NABH (National Accreditation Board for Hospitals & Healthcare Providers), a constituent board of QCI, accredits hospitals in India against defined quality and patient-safety standards. The current hospital standard is the 5th edition.

How many chapters are there in NABH?

The NABH 5th edition hospital standard is organised into 10 chapters covering patient-centred and organisation-centred standards, from Access & Assessment to Information Management.

What score means we are ready for a NABH assessment?

As a rough guide, 85%+ with no open critical gaps suggests you are close to assessment-ready; 70–84% means near ready; below 70% indicates significant preparation is still needed. A formal gap audit is recommended before applying.

Does this replace a formal NABH gap audit?

No. This is a quick self-assessment for prioritisation. A formal, standard-by-standard gap audit by qualified consultants is needed before applying for accreditation.
