FAQ

How many ambulances does a hospital need?

A common planning norm is roughly one ambulance per 100–150 beds, with a minimum of one, scaled up for trauma and emergency load.

What is the difference between BLS and ALS ambulances?

BLS (Basic Life Support) handles stable transport; ALS (Advanced Life Support) carries a ventilator, monitor and critical-care equipment.

Should shifts be considered?

Yes, 24×7 cover needs multiple crews per vehicle and a buffer for maintenance and simultaneous calls.
